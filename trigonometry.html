Introduction to Trigonometry

Trigonometry is a branch of mathematics that studies relationships between side lengths and angles of triangles. It has applications in physics, engineering, astronomy, and many other fields.

Basic Trigonometric Ratios

For a right-angled triangle with angle θ:

  • Sine (sinθ) = Opposite / Hypotenuse
  • Cosine (cosθ) = Adjacent / Hypotenuse
  • Tangent (tanθ) = Opposite / Adjacent

Key Concepts

  • Unit Circle: Defines trigonometric functions for all angles
  • Pythagorean Theorem: a² + b² = c² for right triangles
  • Trigonometric Identities: Equations true for all angle values
  • Law of Sines & Cosines: For solving non-right triangles

Trigonometric Functions & Graphs

The six trigonometric functions (sine, cosine, tangent, cosecant, secant, cotangent) are periodic functions that repeat their values in regular intervals.

Important Identities

Pythagorean Identity: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1

Angle Sum Formulas:

sin(A+B) = sinAcosB + cosAsinB

cos(A+B) = cosAcosB - sinAsinB
